Granola is a Mac-native prosumer meeting note tool targeting individual power users (founders, VCs, PMs) via design quality and a collaborative human-AI note-taking model ("enhanced notes"). Primary source: `competitive.md`.

**Granola wins on:** Design and UX trust among Mac prosumers. Word-of-mouth in tech. No false automation promises.

**Granola loses on:** Platform reach (Mac-only, no Windows/web/API), zero team features, no integrations, no enterprise play, small team with slow velocity.

**MeetFlow threat level (per competitive.md):** High for power users. Granola competes on experience, not features. Risk escalates sharply if Granola adds team features or a web app.

**Key intersection with MeetFlow gaps:** Granola's UX trust advantage is amplified by MeetFlow's 66% action item accuracy and false "high confidence" labels. Sarah Chen (interview-01) is the canonical at-risk power user: 8-10 meetings/day, $4,700/yr Team upgrade blocked entirely by accuracy, not by Granola's design. Fixing Action Item Confidence Scoring v2 (active sprint 17-03-2026) is the primary Granola counter-move.

**Strategic implication already surfaced:** Treat accuracy work as a Granola counter-move and position reliable AI output as the moat design cannot replicate. See `outputs/competitor-snapshot-granola.md`.

**Intel gaps:** No pricing data for Granola on file. No user count or revenue data. No funding information. Primary research needed to fill these.
