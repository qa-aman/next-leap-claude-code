## Run: 17-03-2026

### Initial clusters (pre-Gate 2)

5 clusters emerged before singleton check:
1. Action Item Accuracy (interviews 01, 04)
2. Missing Workflow Integration (interviews 02, 03, 04)
3. Data Privacy and Security Governance (interview 02 only -- singleton)
4. Summary Length and Output Format (interview 03 only -- singleton)
5. Price-to-Value Mismatch (interview 03 only -- singleton)
6. Competitive Displacement (interviews 03, 04)

### Merges performed

- Privacy/Governance (singleton, Marcus only) merged into a broader "Trust in the System" theme alongside Sarah's AI trust erosion. Rationale: both are trust failures at different product layers (infrastructure vs. AI output). Distinct enough to name clearly but related enough to cluster. Source: interviews 01, 02.
- Summary Length (singleton, Priya only) folded into Action Item Accuracy as "AI Output Quality." Rationale: both are failures of structured AI output to deliver value. Theme renamed to "Action Item Accuracy and AI Output Quality." Source: interviews 01, 03, 04.
- Price-to-Value (singleton, Priya only) folded into Competitive Displacement. Rationale: Priya's price sensitivity is the mechanism accelerating her switching risk to Notion. The two pains are causally linked. Source: interviews 03, 04.

### Final clusters (post-Gate 2)

4 themes, all backed by 2+ interviews. No singletons.

### What worked

- Treating "trust" as an umbrella (AI trust + data governance trust) preserved Marcus's unique enterprise concern without creating a singleton.
- Folding summary length into accuracy worked because both are dimensions of "the AI output isn't usable as-is."
- Price sensitivity belonging to competitive displacement kept the framing action-oriented rather than isolated as a pricing problem.

### Watch out for

- If a future round adds more enterprise users, Data Privacy may earn enough mentions to break back out as its own theme.
- "Low active adoption" is a surface symptom, not a root cause -- do not create a theme around it without understanding whether it's driven by integration gaps, trust failures, or something new.
