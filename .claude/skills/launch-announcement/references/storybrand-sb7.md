# Layer 2 Reference - StoryBrand SB7 Framework

From "Building a StoryBrand" (Donald Miller). The customer is the hero. The product is the guide. Customers do not care about the company's story; they care about their own.

## The Seven Parts

1. **Character** - the customer (the primary persona from Layer 1) who wants something.
2. **Problem** - what blocks them. Name the external problem (the task) and the internal problem (how it feels - distrust, wasted time, looking unreliable).
3. **Guide** - the product. The guide shows empathy ("we know this is broken for you") and competence (proof it works). The guide never takes center stage.
4. **Plan** - 2-3 simple steps showing how easy it is to start. Removes the fear of trying.
5. **Call to Action** - one direct ask. "Try it", "Turn it on", "Open your next summary". One CTA per output, never two.
6. **Failure (stakes)** - what staying with the status quo costs them. Use sparingly, one line.
7. **Success** - the after-state. Paint what their work looks like when the problem is gone.

## Mapping SB7 To Each Output Format

Each format compresses the arc differently. Never drop the Problem or the CTA.

| Format | Parts used | Shape |
|---|---|---|
| Announcement blurb | All 7 | Problem -> empathy -> plan -> CTA -> success |
| Customer email | 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 7 | Subject = problem or success. Body opens on THEIR problem, one CTA |
| In-app banner | 2, 4, 5 | Problem + what to do, CTA on the button |
| Social post | 2, 6, 7 | Pain or stakes hook, success close |

## Hero Test

Before finalizing any output, run this test: count the sentences whose subject is "we", "MeetFlow", or the feature name versus sentences whose subject is "you" or the customer's work. The customer must win. This is a test of the subject of the sentence, not of specific phrases - any opener whose grammatical subject is the company or the product fails, no matter how it is worded. Openers that fail instantly:

- "We're excited to announce..."
- "Introducing [Feature]..."
- "MeetFlow now supports..."
- "Today we're shipping..."
- "Starting today, MeetFlow will..."

Rewrites that pass:

- "You leave a meeting with ten action items. Three of them are wrong."
- "Your follow-up email, drafted before you're back at your desk."

## Empathy Before Competence

When the guide (product) appears, lead with empathy, then proof. Proof must be a real number from a repo file with its source noted, never an invented stat.
