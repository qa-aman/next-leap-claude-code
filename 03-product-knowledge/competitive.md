# MeetFlow — Competitive Landscape

---

## Otter.ai

**What they do well:** Market leader in brand recognition. Generous free tier (300 minutes/month) drives massive top-of-funnel. Real-time transcription is best-in-class. Mobile app is polished.

**What they do poorly:** AI summaries are generic — more "transcript highlights" than true synthesis. Action item extraction is basic with no confidence scoring. Enterprise features lag behind. Integrations are limited compared to newer players.

**Who they win with:** Individual users who want free transcription. Students and journalists. Price-sensitive teams that don't need deep AI features.

**Threat level:** Medium. They own the "free meeting transcription" mental model. MeetFlow wins on AI quality and integrations, but if Otter improves their AI layer, their distribution advantage is formidable.

---

## Fireflies.ai

**What they do well:** 100+ integrations — the deepest integration ecosystem in the space. CRM sync (Salesforce, HubSpot) is a key differentiator. Sales teams love the "auto-log calls to CRM" workflow. API is well-documented for custom workflows.

**What they do poorly:** UI feels cluttered and enterprise-focused. Summary quality is mid-tier. Pricing is confusing with multiple add-on tiers. Not a product you'd recommend to a friend — it's a product procurement teams buy.

**Who they win with:** Sales teams and revenue operations. Companies with complex CRM workflows. Organizations that need 100+ integrations.

**Threat level:** High for enterprise pipeline. Fireflies' Salesforce integration is the #1 reason sales-heavy teams consider switching from MeetFlow. Dana's team is building Salesforce sync for Q2 2026, but Fireflies has a 2-year head start.

---

## Granola

**What they do well:** Beautiful, Mac-native experience. The UX feels like a premium product — clean typography, thoughtful interactions, fast. Prosumer positioning with strong word-of-mouth among tech workers and founders. Their "enhanced notes" approach lets users write alongside the AI, creating a collaborative note-taking experience rather than pure automation.

**What they do poorly:** Mac-only (no Windows, no web). No team features. Limited integrations. No API. Small team, slow feature velocity. No enterprise play.

**Who they win with:** Individual power users, especially in tech. Founders, VCs, product managers who value design and are willing to pay for a premium solo experience.

**Threat level:** High for power users. Granola is winning the hearts of exactly the users MeetFlow needs most — engaged power users willing to pay. They don't compete on features; they compete on experience. If Granola adds team features or a web app, they become a direct threat to MeetFlow's Pro tier.

---

## Notion AI

**What they do well:** Bundled free with Notion — zero incremental cost for existing Notion users. Meeting summaries integrate directly into Notion pages alongside project docs, wikis, and tasks. The workflow is seamless: meeting notes live where work already happens.

**What they do poorly:** Not a standalone meeting tool — requires Notion ecosystem buy-in. Meeting AI features are basic compared to dedicated tools. No live recording or real-time transcription. No speaker diarization. No integrations outside Notion.

**Who they win with:** Teams already deep in Notion. Casual meeting note users who want "good enough" summaries without another subscription. Users who value workflow integration over AI quality.

**Threat level:** Existential for the free tier and casual users. Priya-type users (casual, already in Notion, price-sensitive) will switch the moment Notion's meeting features reach "good enough." MeetFlow's free tier exists to convert users to Pro — if Notion captures that casual segment, the conversion funnel breaks.

---

## Where MeetFlow Wins

MeetFlow's advantage is the combination of AI quality + integrations + team features that no single competitor matches. Otter has reach but weak AI. Fireflies has integrations but poor UX. Granola has UX but no team play. Notion has distribution but basic features.

The risk is that MeetFlow's AI quality advantage — specifically action item accuracy — is eroding trust with the power users who drive Pro and Team revenue. If the 34% error rate on action items isn't fixed, the "AI quality" positioning becomes a liability rather than an asset. The competitive moat depends on making the AI features reliable enough that users trust them as their system of record.
