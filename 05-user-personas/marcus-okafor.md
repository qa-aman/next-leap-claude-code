# Marcus Okafor - "The Skeptic"

## Quick Snapshot

- **Role:** Engineering Manager
- **Age:** 41
- **Company:** Enterprise fintech, 2,000 employees
- **Plan:** Enterprise (company-paid, IT-deployed)

## A Typical Day

- You run 4-5 meetings: standups, sprint reviews, 1:1s.
- Your team takes notes in Google Docs - not MeetFlow.
- MeetFlow is installed across your org. You didn't choose it.
- You use it for compliance logging, not as a productivity tool.

## How You Use MeetFlow

- Passively - the recording happens, you don't actively engage.
- You acknowledge transcription is "fine" for compliance purposes.
- Your team ignores the AI summaries and works around the tool.
- You are a vocal critic internally when renewal conversations come up.

## What You Love

- Transcription accuracy is acceptable for compliance records.
- That's the full list.

## What Frustrates You

> "I don't trust any system that records my engineering reviews. We discuss production incidents, security vulnerabilities, compensation. Where does that data go?"

- No clarity on where recordings and transcripts are stored.
- No on-premises storage option for sensitive engineering conversations.
- No SOC 2 Type II certification visible to you or your security team.
- No auto-delete policy (you want 30-day maximum retention).
- Privacy concerns block any meaningful adoption on your team.

## What Would Make You Happy

- On-prem or single-tenant storage for Enterprise accounts.
- Verified SOC 2 Type II certification.
- Configurable auto-delete: 30 days maximum.
- Clear, written data handling documentation you can share with your CISO.

## Revenue Potential

- Your company already pays Enterprise pricing.
- Revenue is not the issue - renewal is.
- You influence whether your company renews or drops the contract.

## Churn Risk

**Medium** - the company pays, so it persists unless you escalate. Your internal detractor status is the real risk. If you block renewal at the next review, the contract ends.
