# Priya Nair - "The Casual"

## Quick Snapshot

- **Role:** Chief of Staff
- **Age:** 29
- **Company:** Growth-stage startup, 45 employees
- **Plan:** Pro ($15/mo), solo account

## A Typical Day

- You attend 2-3 meetings per week - not per day.
- Most are high-stakes: exec syncs and board prep sessions.
- You spend more time communicating outcomes than attending meetings.
- Notion is your primary workspace for everything else.

## How You Use MeetFlow

- Let it run in the background during meetings.
- Post-meeting, summaries auto-push to your #exec-updates Slack channel.
- Your CEO reads the digest every morning - that's your primary output.
- You don't review transcripts or action items in detail.

## What You Love

> "I just love that I don't have to take notes anymore. The Slack digest is perfect - my CEO reads it every morning."

- The Slack integration removes a manual step from your workflow.
- Your CEO gets timely, consistent updates without you writing a word.
- The setup was simple and the habit stuck immediately.

## What Frustrates You

- Summaries are too long. You want 3-4 bullets, not 350 words.
- Your CEO skims - wall-of-text summaries miss the point.
- $15/mo feels expensive for 2-3 uses per week.
- The value-to-cost ratio doesn't feel right at low usage volume.

## What Would Make You Happy

- A "brief mode" summary: 3-4 bullets maximum, no padding.
- Configurable summary length per user or per channel.
- A lower-tier plan priced for light usage (under $10/mo).

## Revenue Potential

- Currently paying $180/yr on Pro.
- No upgrade path discussed - usage doesn't justify Team plan.
- Notion is her primary tool and already the threat replacement.

## Churn Risk

**High** - light usage means low switching cost. If Notion adds meeting summaries, she leaves. Price sensitivity compounds this. No deep integration keeping her locked in.
