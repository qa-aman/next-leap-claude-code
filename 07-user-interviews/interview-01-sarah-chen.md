# User Interview — Sarah Chen

**Date:** January 14, 2026
**Duration:** 45 minutes
**Interviewer:** Aman Parmar (PM, AI Intelligence)
**Persona:** Power User (Head of Product, Series C startup)

---

## Context

Sarah has been a MeetFlow Pro subscriber for 11 months. She runs 8-10 meetings per day and uses action items as her primary task management system. She was flagged by customer success as a "high-value at-risk" user after submitting 3 support tickets about action item accuracy in December 2025.

---

## Key Themes

### 1. Action item accuracy is the core pain

Sarah relies on MeetFlow's action items as her to-do list. When items are wrong or missing, it breaks her entire workflow.

> "I double-check the action items manually. Which defeats the purpose. If I'm going to review every item anyway, why not just take notes myself?"

> "Confidence score says 'high' but misses the most important item. Every. Single. Time. Yesterday it caught 'send the invite' but missed 'we need to realign the roadmap before board review.' That's the one that matters."

She estimates she catches 2-3 errors per day across her meetings. On a heavy day (10 meetings), that's 30 minutes of manual correction — eating into the time MeetFlow is supposed to save.

### 2. Implicit commitments are the blind spot

The biggest accuracy gap is with action items that aren't stated as direct commands.

> "When someone says 'I'll circle back on that,' that's a commitment. MeetFlow doesn't catch it. When my CEO says 'let's think about whether we should revisit pricing,' that's an action item for me. MeetFlow sees it as a discussion point."

### 3. Trust erosion affects adoption of new features

Sarah hasn't turned on Meeting Pattern Insights (beta) even though she's the target user.

> "I don't trust the AI enough to give me meeting analytics. If it can't get action items right, why would I trust it to tell me how I spend my time?"

### 4. Willingness to pay is high — conditional on accuracy

> "Fix accuracy, I'll move my whole team to Team plan. That's 8 seats. I've already pitched it to my CEO, but I told him we need to wait until the action items are reliable."

---

## Researcher Notes

Sarah represents the highest-value conversion opportunity: a single power user who could drive $4,700/year in Team plan revenue. But the conversion is blocked entirely by action item accuracy.

This isn't a feature request — it's a ceiling on willingness to pay. No amount of new features (Smart Follow-Up, Insights, integrations) will move users like Sarah until the core product delivers on its promise.

The implicit commitment gap she describes aligns with the known model weakness documented in PRODUCT.md. This should be the #1 priority for Action Item Scoring v2.
