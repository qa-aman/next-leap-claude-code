# User Interview — Marcus Okafor

**Date:** January 21, 2026
**Duration:** 30 minutes (originally scheduled for 45, cut short)
**Interviewer:** Aman Parmar (PM, AI Intelligence)
**Persona:** Skeptic (Engineering Manager, Enterprise fintech)

---

## Context

Marcus's company deployed MeetFlow org-wide (Team plan, 120 seats) six months ago. IT made the purchasing decision. Marcus agreed to the interview reluctantly after his VP of Engineering asked him to participate. He was direct about not being a fan of the product.

---

## Key Themes

### 1. Privacy is the dealbreaker, not features

Marcus's concerns are structural, not about AI quality.

> "I don't trust any system that records my engineering reviews. We discuss production incidents, security vulnerabilities, sometimes compensation. Where does that data go? Who at MeetFlow can access it?"

> "I asked IT about the data retention policy. They didn't have a clear answer. That's a problem."

He wants: SOC 2 Type II certification, on-prem transcript storage, configurable auto-delete (30/60/90 days), and the ability to exclude specific meetings from recording.

### 2. Passive usage, not active adoption

> "My team uses it because IT deployed it. Nobody reads the summaries. We have our own Google Docs process for sprint reviews and retros. MeetFlow runs in the background — I forget it's there most days."

When asked if any features would change his mind:

> "Honestly? Probably not. I don't think meetings need AI. I think meetings need to be shorter and fewer. But if you're going to record them, at least let me control where the data lives."

### 3. Influence on contract renewal

Marcus is on the engineering leadership team (8 managers, reporting to the VP). His org accounts for 40 of the 120 Team seats. If he recommends against renewal, it's a meaningful signal.

> "When the contract comes up, I'll tell [VP] that we're paying for a tool most of my team ignores. That's not a quality issue — it's an adoption issue. And adoption won't happen until I trust the security story."

---

## Researcher Notes

Marcus is not the target user for AI Intelligence features. He won't be convinced by better action items or smarter summaries — his objection is at the infrastructure and compliance layer.

However, his feedback validates the Enterprise tier roadmap (Tomás's scope): SOC 2, on-prem storage, and granular admin controls would neutralize his objections and protect the 120-seat contract.

For the AI Intelligence pillar specifically, Marcus is a "leave alone" — don't invest in converting skeptics, invest in delighting power users like Sarah who already believe in the product.
