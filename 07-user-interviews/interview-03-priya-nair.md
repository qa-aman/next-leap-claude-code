# User Interview — Priya Nair

**Date:** January 28, 2026
**Duration:** 35 minutes
**Interviewer:** Aman Parmar (PM, AI Intelligence)
**Persona:** Casual (Chief of Staff, Growth-stage startup)

---

## Context

Priya has been a MeetFlow Pro subscriber for 5 months. She uses it for 2-3 meetings per week, primarily exec syncs and board prep sessions. She was recruited through an in-app prompt after giving an NPS score of 8.

---

## Key Themes

### 1. Slack integration is the core value

Priya doesn't open MeetFlow's app. Her entire experience is through the Slack digest.

> "I just love that I don't have to take notes anymore. The summary shows up in #exec-updates and my CEO reads it every morning. That's the whole value for me."

> "If the Slack integration broke, I'd probably cancel within a week. That's how central it is to my workflow."

### 2. Summaries are too long

> "The summaries are, like, 6 paragraphs? I skim them. My CEO skims them. I want 3-4 bullet points — the decisions that were made and who's doing what. That's it."

She doesn't use action items as a task system (unlike Sarah). For Priya, action items are just part of the summary she skims.

### 3. Price sensitivity

> "Fifteen dollars a month for something I use twice a week... I keep it because the Slack thing is so convenient, but it feels like a lot. If it were $8, I wouldn't think about it."

When asked about the Team plan:

> "We're only 45 people. The $49/seat price is a non-starter. My CEO would ask why we're paying for meeting notes when we could just assign someone to take them."

### 4. Notion is the quiet threat

> "We live in Notion. Everything — docs, projects, OKRs. If Notion added meeting summaries that auto-posted into our meeting notes pages... honestly, I'd switch. It would just make more sense to have everything in one place."

She's aware of Notion AI but hasn't tried the meeting features yet. "I heard they're adding it. I'll check it out when it launches."

---

## Researcher Notes

Priya represents a large segment of casual users: high satisfaction, low depth. She loves one feature (Slack digest) but isn't deeply embedded in the product. This makes her highly vulnerable to:

1. Notion bundling meeting features (her primary tool already)
2. Price increases or removal of features from the Pro tier
3. Any Slack integration instability

For the AI Intelligence pillar: Priya's feedback on summary length validates the "summaries too long" signal from the Q1 survey (14 mentions). Shorter, more structured summaries would improve her experience. But she's not the user to build for — she'll churn to Notion regardless if the bundling play works.

The strategic question is whether users like Priya (casual, price-sensitive, single-feature-dependent) are worth retaining, or whether MeetFlow should focus retention efforts on power users like Sarah who drive higher ARPU and are more defensible against bundling.
