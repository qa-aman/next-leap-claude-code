# User Interview — James Whitfield

**Date:** February 4, 2026
**Duration:** 40 minutes
**Interviewer:** Aman Parmar (PM, AI Intelligence)
**Persona:** Sales Director (Mid-market SaaS, 200-person company)

---

## Context

James leads a 15-person sales team. His company is on MeetFlow's Team plan (22 seats across sales and customer success). He was referred by the account manager after mentioning in a QBR that his team was evaluating Fireflies as a replacement.

---

## Key Themes

### 1. Salesforce integration is non-negotiable

James's entire pipeline lives in Salesforce. Meeting notes that don't end up in Salesforce don't exist in his world.

> "I need this in Salesforce. That's where my pipeline lives. When my rep has a call with a prospect, I need the summary and action items in the opportunity record. Not in Slack, not in Notion — in Salesforce."

> "Right now my team manually copies meeting summaries into Salesforce. That's 10-15 minutes per call. With 15 reps doing 4-5 calls a day, that's 10+ hours of daily admin work across the team."

### 2. Fireflies is the active competitive threat

> "Fireflies does the Salesforce thing. Auto-logs the call summary, action items, even sentiment analysis right into the opportunity. We're considering switching."

> "I don't want to switch. MeetFlow's summaries are better than Fireflies. But 'better summaries' doesn't beat 'summaries that are actually in Salesforce.' Integration wins over quality when quality isn't in my workflow."

He's given his team until end of Q1 2026 to make a decision. If MeetFlow doesn't have a Salesforce timeline by then, they'll start a Fireflies pilot.

### 3. Action item accuracy matters for sales follow-ups

James uses action items differently than Sarah — not as a task list, but as follow-up prompts.

> "After a prospect call, I check the action items to make sure my rep committed to the right next steps. If MeetFlow says 'send pricing proposal by Friday' and the rep actually said 'send case studies first, then pricing next week,' that's a problem. My rep looks unprofessional."

> "The accuracy thing is real. Maybe 1 in 3 calls has an action item that's wrong enough to matter. For sales, wrong follow-up is worse than no follow-up."

### 4. Team plan value perception

> "The Team plan is worth it for us — $49/seat is nothing compared to the time savings. But only if the tool integrates with our stack. Right now it's a standalone island."
