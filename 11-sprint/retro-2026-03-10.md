# Sprint Retrospective - March 10, 2026

**Date:** March 10, 2026
**Sprint covered:** February 25 - March 10, 2026
**Facilitator:** You
**Attendees:** Kai, Remi, Yuki, Priscilla, Dev, Noor

---

## What Went Well

- We shipped the confidence score tooltip on time. Small change, but it surfaced data that was completely hidden before.
- Remi's labeled data audit was fast. We now know exactly where the model gaps are - implicit commitments and conditional actions. That clarity saved a week of guessing.
- Communication between ML and frontend was smoother this sprint. The shared Slack channel for API contract updates actually got used.
- Noor's design prototypes were ready before engineering needed them. That almost never happens.

---

## What Didn't Go Well

- The tooltip UI shipped but the underlying score quality is still the problem. We shipped the display, not the fix. Users will see confidence scores that are not reliable yet.
- Priscilla was blocked for two days waiting on DB schema sign-off. That should have been resolved in sprint planning.
- We took on too many story points. We committed to 42 and completed 31. The carry-over items will roll into the next sprint and create pressure.
- Retro was scheduled for 90 minutes. We ran 2 hours. No one facilitates time well.

---

## What We'll Change

- Cap story points at 32 per sprint until we have two sprints in a row where we finish everything committed. Velocity before ambition.
- Any infrastructure decision (schema changes, API contracts) gets resolved in sprint planning - not during the sprint. If it is not resolved, it goes on the backlog.
- Retro gets a hard 60-minute limit with a timer. Kai will facilitate next time so the PM isn't doing it.
