Usability / UX flow - you 2/5, IKEA 4/5

Mark finds your material copy, but hits dead ends on reviews and assembly, and a spec contradiction stops him mid-check.

Customer rating and reviews section

Conversion lever: decision confidence / social proof

IKEA
Shows "4.6 (34)" stars directly under the price, plus a full "Customer reviews" section with a "Show all reviews" button.
You
No rating, no review count, no reviews section anywhere. A stray "0.0" sits by the Wishlist icon and can be misread as a zero rating.
Gap
A cautious buyer's first move is to read what others said. With nothing there, Mark has no social proof and stalls. (Nielsen: visibility of system status.)
Fix
Add a star rating by the price and a reviews block below the description, seeded from your marketplace reviews. Remove or relabel the stray "0.0". See ticket A2.

Assembly guidance

Conversion lever: friction / effort certainty

IKEA
An "Assembly and documents" section with a downloadable manual, a "What's included" tab listing parts, and an optional paid Assembly Service.
You

Fix
Add an "Assembly" line: estimated time, tools, part count, and a link to the PDF manual. See ticket A3.

Prove it: experiment plan for the top 2 bets

Benchmarks are industry averages. These tests turn them into your numbers, so you can defend the investment.

Bet
Ratings + reviews on the PDP (A2)
Hypothesis
Showing a star rating and reviews on the PDP raises add-to-cart rate for considered-purchase categories.
Metric
Primary: PDP → add-to-cart rate. Secondary: PDP → purchase. Guardrail: return rate.
Design
A/B on a category cohort. Control = no reviews; variant = rating chip + reviews section. Split traffic 50/50.
MDE
Power the test to detect a conservative +5% relative lift in add-to-cart at 95% confidence, even though the benchmark implies far more. Watch the 4.0-4.7 star sweet spot - suppress or contextualise very low-rated SKUs.
Bet
Copy accuracy - fixing spec contradictions (A1)
Hypothesis
Removing description/spec contradictions reduces bounce and wrong-size returns and lifts add-to-cart on affected PDPs.
Metric
Primary: add-to-cart rate on corrected PDPs. Secondary: bounce rate and "wrong item/size" return rate.
Design
This is a defect fix, so a pre/post on a cohort of corrected PDPs is enough; keep a small holdout if you want a clean read. Track returns for 60-90 days post-fix.
MDE
Target a measurable drop in wrong-size returns on corrected SKUs; even a small reduction pays back the low effort many times over.
