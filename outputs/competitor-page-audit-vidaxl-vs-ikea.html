You trail on every lens. The widest gap is feature parity: IKEA's page carries the trust and detail elements a cautious buyer needs, and yours is missing most of them. Note the irony - your cabinet is solid pine, IKEA's is particleboard. The product is arguably better; the page tells a weaker story.

Usability / UX flow - you 2/5, IKEA 4/5

Mark can find your material copy, but hits dead ends on reviews and assembly - two of his three goals - and a spec contradiction stops him mid-check.

Customer rating and reviews section

IKEA
Shows "4.6 (34)" stars directly under the price, plus a full "Customer reviews" section with a "Show all reviews" button.
You
No rating, no review count, no reviews section anywhere. A stray "0.0" sits by the Wishlist icon and can be misread as a zero rating.
Gap
A cautious buyer's first move is to read what others said. With nothing there, Mark has no social proof and stalls. (Nielsen: visibility of system status.)
Fix
Add a star rating by the price and a reviews block below the description, seeded from your marketplace reviews. Remove or relabel the stray "0.0" so it is not mistaken for a score.

Assembly guidance

IKEA
An "Assembly and documents" section with a downloadable manual, a "What's included" tab listing parts, and an optional paid Assembly Service.
You
Specs state "Assembly required: Yes" and nothing more - no manual, no time estimate, no tools list.
Gap
Mark wants to judge effort before he commits. "Yes" with no detail leaves him guessing, so he hesitates at the buy button. (Nielsen: help and documentation.)
Fix
Add an "Assembly" line: estimated time, tools needed, number of parts, and a link to the PDF manual. This answers the exact question blocking his add-to-cart.

Feature / attribute parity - you 2/5, IKEA 5/5

The elements a durable-goods buyer expects are largely missing from your page and all present on IKEA's.

Warranty statement

IKEA
"10 year warranty" listed under Product features.
You
No warranty mentioned anywhere on the page.
Gap

Fix
State the warranty period near the price or in specs. If you offer a guarantee, surface it as a labeled trust badge in the buy box.

You

Fix
Add a one-line returns statement beside Add to Cart (e.g. "30-day returns") linking to the full policy.

Messaging & copy - you 3/5, IKEA 4/5

Your material copy is actually rich and benefit-led. One factual contradiction undermines all of it.

Description vs Specifications CRITICAL

IKEA
Title, description, and measurements tab all agree: 60x32x60 cm, one shelf and door.
You
Your Description says "Measuring a convenient 60 x 40 x 80 cm" and "three adjustable shelves," while the title and Specifications say "35 x 30 x 60 cm" and "With 1 adjustable shelf."
Gap
Contradictory dimensions and shelf count are trust-killing for a detail-driven buyer. Mark cannot tell which is true and may abandon rather than risk it. (Nielsen: consistency and standards; error prevention.)
Fix
Rewrite the description to match the real specs (35x30x60 cm, 1 shelf). This is a copy defect, not a design choice - fix it before anything else on this list.

Header search placeholder text

IKEA
"What are you looking for?" - invites a query - plus a search-by-photo option.
You
Placeholder reads "Find the next purchase" - brand-speak that does not tell the user what to type.
Gap
The placeholder is the one spot to teach search. "Find the next purchase" teaches nothing, so a hesitant user doesn't know it accepts an item number. (Nielsen: match between system and the real world.) This is the concrete answer to your original "what do I even change on the search bar" question - it's the placeholder copy.
Fix
Change the placeholder to "Search products or item number," matching what the box actually accepts.

Visual & conversion design - you 3/5, IKEA 4/5

Your buy box is clean and your imagery is strong. Two things quietly cost you a cautious buyer's confidence.

Trust anchor above the fold

IKEA
The 4.6 star rating sits directly under the price, anchoring trust before any scroll.
You
Above the fold shows price, colour, and Add to Cart, but no rating or trust badge. Trust items (secure checkout, free shipping) sit lower down.
Gap
The first screen is where Mark decides whether to keep going. With no trust anchor there, he has less reason to commit.
Fix
Once reviews exist, surface the rating into the buy box, plus one trust badge (returns or secure checkout) near the price.

"Items bought by others" recommendations

IKEA
"Related products," "Explore more options," and "Get the look" all show ENHET-range parts - doors, organisers, legs - relevant to the cabinet.
You
"Items bought by others" shows tent pegs, garden chairs, sandbags, a cat scratching post, and a Christmas cactus - unrelated to a bathroom cabinet.
Gap
Irrelevant cross-sell signals a generic, untargeted store and wastes space that could upsell matching bathroom pieces. It quietly erodes a cautious buyer's confidence.
Fix
Replace the generic carousel with same-range or bathroom items - your "Frequently Bought Together" already surfaces the matching FLORO drawer cabinet. Relevance both reassures and lifts basket size.
