## Post 3 - Use-Case

**Post type:** Use-case. A day in the life of a Head of Product running 8-10 meetings. Anonymized, not a named customer.

**Copy:**

8 to 10 back-to-back meetings. No buffer. By 6pm, no energy left to clean up what the AI got wrong.

This is a real pattern we hear from heads of product who live in their calendar. The AI action items are their task list, so when an item is missing, something real falls through. They cope by re-checking every output by hand, which defeats the point of having an assistant at all.

Collaborative Live Notes is built for exactly this day.

During the call, they jot a line in the notes pane: "I own the pricing follow-up." One highlight turns it into a tracked action item, guaranteed, no waiting on the model to infer it.

A suggested action item looks off mid-meeting. They edit it inline and move on.

By the time the next call starts, the record is already correct. No evening cleanup. No second-guessing the list.

The fix was not a smarter summary after the fact. It was giving people the pen while the meeting is still live.

## Post 4 - Differentiation

**Post type:** Differentiation. Writing alongside the AI versus pure post-meeting automation.

**Copy:**

Most meeting AI hands you a summary after the call and hopes it is right.

We rebuilt the moment that actually matters: while the meeting is still happening.

Pure post-meeting automation has a structural flaw. It captures everything, then asks you to trust the output later, with no way to intervene while the context is fresh. When action items come back wrong or missing 34% of the time, "review it afterward" is not a workflow, it is a second job.

Collaborative Live Notes takes a different shape. You and the AI write into the same meeting at the same time. The AI streams the transcript and suggests action items as they surface. You type your own notes alongside, correct the AI inline, and lock in the lines you care about by hand.

Automation captures. Collaboration captures and corrects, together, in real time.

That is the difference between a transcript you hope is right and a record you know is right.
